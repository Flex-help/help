Parcel Types

Amazon defines three main parcel types. Be aware: the app sometimes labels parcels incorrectly, so you may see a small box marked as an envelope or poly bag/ vice versa. If you can’t identify the parcel type, use the stop number indicator on the label instead.

  • General Parcels: These range from small plastic bags with items like socks, to medium and large boxes, and even bulky items like lawnmowers. Amazon usually categorises with (s) for small, (l) for large, and (xl) for extra-large or heavy parcels.

Driver Aid

Ignore everything else on this label — the key piece of information is the number in the bottom right corner, marked Driver Aid. This number tells you which stop the parcel is for:

  • Lower numbers are delivered earlier.
  • Higher numbers are delivered later.

Ideally, your parcels should always be numbered in sequence (1 up to however many parcels you have). But if the parcels have been resorted without being re-labelled, the previous drivers stop numbers will still be on them — leaving you with a random jumble that isn’t in order.

If this happens, sticking to the boot loading by parcel type method will still give you some structure and help speed things up, even without reliable driver aid numbers.

Loading your boot

Organise your boot in two ways:

  1. By parcel type
  2. By delivery order

At each stop you’ll have two main identifiers: the parcel type and the parcel number. Split your boot into sections, stack your boot so that higher numbers are at the bottom of each pile, and lower numbers are on top. This way, the parcels you need first are always the easiest to grab.

Tip: The cage bags you’re given are usually partially ordered — for example, one bag might have parcels 1–15 and another 16–30. Load the higher-numbered bags first (at the bottom), then stack the lower numbers on top. This keeps your car organised and speeds up each stop.
